Who we are

CABANA Collections (ABN 91 302 503 433) (“CABANA”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is an Australian sole-trader e-commerce business based in Canberra, ACT. We sell online within Australia only.

About this policy

Most Australian small businesses (≤ A$3m turnover) are generally exempt from the Privacy Act. We voluntarily follow the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This document sets out how we handle personal information in line with the APPs. We review this policy at least annually and if our operations change (for example, if we expand internationally or our annual turnover exceeds A$3 million).

What this policy covers

  • What personal information we collect
  • How we use and disclose it
  • Overseas disclosures
  • Security and retention
  • Your privacy choices and rights
  • Marketing & the Spam Act
  • Cookies & Google Analytics
  • Third-party links
  • How to contact us and make a complaint

“Personal information” has the meaning in the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth).

Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Identity & contact: name, email, phone, delivery address.
  • Order & payment: items purchased, order notes, payment status (card details go directly to our payment provider; we don’t store full card numbers).
  • Support & reviews: messages you send us, product reviews or feedback.

Information we collect automatically

  • Device & usage: IP address, approximate location (city/region), browser/OS, pages viewed, time on page, referring sources.
  • Cookies & similar tech: see Cookies & Google Analytics.

Sensitive information

We do not seek to collect sensitive information (e.g., health, biometric, political opinions). Please don’t provide it.

How we use your information

  • Fulfilling orders: processing payments, shipping, returns/exchanges.
  • Customer support: responding to questions and requests.
  • Account & service messages: order confirmations, shipping updates.
  • Marketing (opt-in only): emails about offers/news; you can unsubscribe anytime (see Marketing & the Spam Act).
  • Security & fraud prevention.
  • Improvement & analytics: understanding site usage to improve our website, products, and customer experience.
  • Legal compliance: recordkeeping and lawful requests.

When we disclose personal information

We don’t sell your personal information. We disclose only as needed to:

  • Service providers: hosting, payment processing, shipping/couriers, email/support tools, and analytics (Google Analytics). Providers receive only what’s necessary and must use it securely for our purposes.
  • Professional advisers: accountants, auditors, insurers, lawyers.
  • Legal/regulatory: where required by law or to protect rights/safety.
  • Business change: if we undergo a sale/restructure, customer data may transfer under the same protections.

Overseas disclosures (APP 8)

Some providers may store or access data from outside Australia (e.g., cloud/analytics). In particular, we use Google Analytics provided by Google LLC (USA) to understand website usage. We take reasonable steps so recipients protect it consistently with the APPs (e.g., contractual and technical safeguards).

Security (APP 11)

We use administrative, technical and physical safeguards appropriate to the risk, including TLS/SSL in transit, hardened hosting, access controls/least-privilege, and periodic reviews. No system is 100% secure; if a data incident occurs, we’ll act promptly.

Data breaches (voluntary)

Although many small businesses are exempt from the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme, we aim to act consistently with its principles. If we suspect a breach likely to result in serious harm, we will promptly assess and, where appropriate, notify affected individuals, provide guidance on protective steps, and consider notifying the OAIC.

Retention and deletion

  • Orders & tax records: kept for at least 5 years (we currently retain for 7 years) to meet AU tax/accounting needs.
  • Support emails: generally up to 2 years after resolution (or as needed for disputes).
  • Marketing records: until you unsubscribe (we keep a minimal suppression record to honour the opt-out).
  • Analytics: Google Analytics data is currently set to 26 months.

When no longer needed, we delete or de-identify information.

Your choices and rights

  • Access & correction: request a copy of your personal information and ask us to correct inaccuracies.
  • Deletion: request deletion where we no longer need the information (subject to legal obligations).
  • Marketing opt-out: unsubscribe at any time via the link in our emails or by contacting us.
  • Cookie controls: manage cookies in your browser; see below.

We verify identity for certain requests and respond within a reasonable time.

Marketing & the Spam Act

We only send marketing emails if you opt in. Every marketing message clearly identifies us and includes an unsubscribe that we action within 5 business days and that remains functional for at least 30 days after the message is sent, in line with the Spam Act 2003 (Cth). Messages identify us as the sender (Joshua James trading as CABANA Collections, ABN 91 302 503 433) and include our contact details.

Cookies & Google Analytics

We use cookies to run our site and Google Analytics to understand aggregated usage (e.g., which pages are visited). Google Analytics uses identifiers/cookies to generate reports for us; we do not receive information that directly identifies you.

  • You can block or clear cookies in your browser.
  • To opt out of Google Analytics, install Google’s Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on (tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).

If we later add advertising pixels or change analytics, we’ll update this policy and, where required, obtain consent.

Third-party links

Our website may contain links to third-party sites. Their privacy practices are their own; we recommend reviewing their privacy policies. Once you leave our site (e.g., to a courier tracking page or social platform), their rules apply.

Children

Our site isn’t directed to persons under 16. We don’t knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information, contact us and we’ll delete it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be posted here with a new “Last updated” date.

Complaints

If you’re concerned about our handling of your personal information, please contact us first. If we don’t resolve your complaint within a reasonable time (generally 30 days), you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) to make a privacy complaint: oaic.gov.au.
